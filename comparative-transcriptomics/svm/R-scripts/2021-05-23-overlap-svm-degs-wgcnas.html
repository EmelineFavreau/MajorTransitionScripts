Objective of analysis

We obtained 123 predictor genes from SVM models: these genes are common to all six species ( Ceratina australensis, C. calcarata, Megalopta genalis, Polistes canadensis, P. dominula, Liostenogaster flavolineata). We want to qualify them: are they also differentially expressed between reproductives and non-reproductives? are they also significantly associated with reproductive status in WGCNA?

Analysis steps:

  • Obtaining data
  • Aim 1: Overlap of DEGs and SVM predictor genes
  • Aim 2: Overlap of WGCNAs and SVM predictor genes

Most SVM common predictor genes are differentially expressed in one species only. See the last 5 vertical bars, for instance there are 18 SVM common predictor genes that are differentially expressed in Polistes dominula only.

Out of 132 SVM common predictor genes, there is not much overlap with DEGs. This is expected, because there is not many common DEGs across all species.

Out of 132 SVM common predictor genes, there is a bit of overlap with WGCNA genes. Maybe noteworthy: 15 genes between L. flavolineata and M. genalis; 1 gene between all but P. canadensis; 1 gene between the three bees.
